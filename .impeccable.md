## Design Context

### Users
Anxious patients and caregivers looking for quick, reliable accessibility information. They are often on mobile devices, potentially under stress or time pressure, and their primary goal is to avoid the physical and emotional cost of an inaccessible healthcare visit.

### Brand Personality
**Calm, Reassuring, Precise.**
The interface should feel like a "safe harbor"—stable, trustworthy, and clear. It should reduce anxiety through structured clarity and a "human-clinical" balance: professional enough to trust with health data, but warm enough to feel supportive.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Refined Clinical Minimalism.**
- **Theme**: Light mode default (clean/sterile/safe), with a high-contrast dark mode for light sensitivity.
- **Visual Style**: Apple-style spacing and clarity, Notion-like readability.
- **Typography**: Highly legible sans-serifs with generous leading and strict hierarchy.
- **Anti-References**: Flashy gradients, generic stock photography, playful/bouncy animations, or cluttered dashboard layouts.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity Over Decoration**: Every element must earn its place. If it doesn't aid the user's decision or reduce their stress, it is noise.
2. **Cognitive Load Reduction**: Use progressive disclosure and easy-to-scan layouts to guide users under pressure.
3. **Redundant Signaling**: Never rely on color alone (e.g., icons + text labels). Information must be perceivable by users with varying sensory needs.
4. **Physical Safety**: Large touch targets and high contrast are not just "nice to have"—they are requirements for users who may have limited fine motor control or vision.
5. **Stability**: Minimal, purposeful motion. Decelerate transitions smoothly to maintain a "calm" atmosphere.
